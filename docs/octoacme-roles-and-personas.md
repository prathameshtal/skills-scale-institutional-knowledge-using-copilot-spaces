# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria, provide technical feedback on feasibility
- **With Project Managers**: Report progress, highlight technical risks and dependencies
- **With QA Lead**: Collaborate on test automation and defect resolution
- **With UX Designers**: Implement designs, discuss technical constraints and trade-offs
- **With DevOps Engineers**: Work on CI/CD improvements, troubleshoot deployment issues
- **With Business Analysts**: Seek clarification on business requirements and workflows

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Define requirements, review solutions, make prioritization trade-offs
- **With Project Managers**: Align on roadmap timing, discuss scope and resource constraints
- **With UX Designers**: Collaborate on user experience and validate designs with user research
- **With QA Lead**: Validate quality standards and acceptance criteria
- **With Business Analysts**: Refine business requirements into product features
- **With DevOps Engineers**: Understand infrastructure constraints and deployment considerations

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Align on priorities, manage scope and timeline trade-offs
- **With Developers**: Track progress, remove blockers, facilitate planning sessions
- **With QA Lead**: Monitor testing progress, ensure quality gates are met
- **With UX Designer**: Coordinate design reviews and approval workflows
- **With DevOps Engineers**: Track infrastructure readiness and deployment schedules
- **With Business Analysts**: Ensure requirements clarity and stakeholder alignment

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads ensure quality standards are met throughout the development lifecycle. They design testing strategies, manage test automation, and validate that features meet acceptance criteria before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain testing strategy and quality standards
- Create and manage test plans and test cases
- Lead test automation initiatives and maintain test suites
- Coordinate with Developers on testability and defect resolution
- Validate acceptance criteria with Product Managers
- Report on quality metrics and test coverage
- Participate in release readiness reviews

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal defects
- Increase test automation coverage and efficiency
- Reduce time spent on manual regression testing
- Establish clear quality gates for releases

### Typical Communication
- Daily collaboration with Developers on bug triage
- Weekly quality reports to Project Managers
- Sprint planning and retrospectives with the delivery team
- Release readiness reviews with stakeholders

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Collaborate on test automation, review testability of code, triage and prioritize defects
- **With Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria, provide input on quality risks for features
- **With Project Managers**: Report on testing progress, highlight quality risks and dependencies
- **With DevOps Engineers**: Integrate automated tests into CI/CD pipelines, coordinate on test environment needs

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered designs that are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with product goals. They research user needs, design interfaces, and validate solutions through usability testing.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define information architecture and user flows
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design principles
- Collaborate with Product Managers on user stories and requirements
- Work with Developers to ensure design feasibility and implementation
- Maintain design systems and style guides

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Validate designs through user feedback and testing
- Ensure consistency across product interfaces
- Balance user needs with technical constraints

### Typical Communication
- Regular design reviews with Product Managers and stakeholders
- Collaboration sessions with Developers on implementation
- User research findings and usability test results
- Design specifications and documentation

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Translate product requirements into user experiences, validate solutions with users
- **With Developers**: Provide design specifications, collaborate on implementation feasibility and trade-offs
- **With Business Analysts**: Understand business requirements and user workflows
- **With QA Lead**: Define usability test cases and acceptance criteria for user experience

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, deployment pipelines, and tooling that enable reliable, efficient software delivery. They bridge development and operations to ensure smooth deployments and system reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Automate deployment and operational processes
- Ensure security best practices in infrastructure
- Optimize build times and deployment efficiency
- Support incident response and system reliability

### Goals
- Enable fast, reliable deployments with minimal downtime
- Automate repetitive operational tasks
- Improve system observability and reliability
- Reduce time from commit to production

### Typical Communication
- Daily coordination with Developers on pipeline issues
- Regular infrastructure and deployment planning with Project Managers
- Incident response and postmortem reviews
- Security and compliance reporting to stakeholders

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Provide build and deployment tools, troubleshoot CI/CD issues, optimize build performance
- **With QA Lead**: Integrate automated tests into pipelines, manage test environments
- **With Project Managers**: Report on deployment readiness, infrastructure risks, and capacity planning
- **With Product Managers**: Understand feature requirements that impact infrastructure and deployment

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business stakeholders and technical teams by gathering requirements, analyzing processes, and ensuring solutions deliver business value. They translate business needs into clear, actionable requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create functional specifications and user stories
- Facilitate requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs
- Analyze data to support decision-making
- Maintain requirements traceability and documentation

### Goals
- Ensure clear understanding of business needs across teams
- Deliver solutions that provide measurable business value
- Reduce ambiguity in requirements and acceptance criteria
- Improve efficiency of business processes

### Typical Communication
- Regular stakeholder interviews and requirements sessions
- Collaboration with Product Managers on prioritization
- Documentation of business rules and workflows
- Status updates on requirements clarity and validation

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Collaborate on product vision, translate business needs into product requirements
- **With Developers**: Clarify requirements, provide business context for technical decisions
- **With UX Designers**: Define user workflows and business process requirements
- **With Project Managers**: Report on requirements status, highlight scope and dependency issues

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- These roles work together in cross-functional teams, with clear handoffs and collaboration points throughout the project lifecycle.
